What is an EAN?

EAN, or EAN13, stands for International Article Number (originally European Article Number). It is an extension of the UPC codes and you'll find them as barcodes on most everyday products. Sometimes the barcode is also called GTIN or GTIN13 (Global Trade Identifier).

Which information is included in an EAN?

The EAN code contains the country of origin, the manufacturer, and a product number. It also includes includes a checksum for verification.

When you only look at the barcode, you can't easily see which product it belongs to. This website provides an EAN lookup, that tells you the product the EAN code belongs to.

Is this EAN database complete?

Currently the database includes over 970 million different EANs. That is huge, but still only a subset of all the EANs ever issued.

Can I use a barcode scanner?

Yes, you can use a professional barcode scanner and feed us the scanned barcodes through the API or in an Excel sheet to look up the products in our database. You can also use your smartphone or webcam to scan barcodes online. On Linux, you can use zbarcam-qt or BarcodeScanner to scan and look up barcodes.

Is an ISBN number also a valid EAN code?

You can convert 10-digit ISBN numbers (ISBN-10) into ISBN-13 numbers. The ISBN-13 numbers are valid EAN codes and are located in "bookland" with the prefixes 978 and 979. Our ISBN search automatically does the conversion and shows both versions.
